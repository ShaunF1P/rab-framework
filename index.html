About the RAB Framework

Theoretical Foundation

The Risk Appetite Balancing (RAB) Framework operationalizes concepts from operations management and enterprise risk management into a practical decision-support tool for boards and executives.

Key Concepts Integrated:

  • Efficient Frontier (Markowitz): Organizations strive to position themselves on the efficient frontier, balancing competing performance measures. The RAB framework helps identify this optimal balance in risk-taking.
  • COSO ERM Framework: Aligns with COSO's definition of risk appetite as "the types and amount of risk an organization is willing to accept in pursuit of value."
  • ISO 31000: Distinguishes between Risk Appetite (willing to pursue), Risk Tolerance (acceptable deviation), and Risk Capacity (maximum bearable).
  • Queueing Theory: Balancing waiting cost against capacity cost — a fundamental risk tradeoff in operational decisions.
  • Newsvendor Model: Balancing underage cost (missed opportunity) against overage cost (excessive commitment) to inform the recommendation engine.

Assessment Methodology

The framework uses 20 scenario-based questions distributed across five dimensions (4 questions per dimension). Each question presents a realistic scenario with a 5-point Likert scale from conservative to aggressive.

Scoring:

  • Dimension Score = ((Average Response - 1) / 4) x 100
  • Overall RAB Score = Sum (Dimension Score x Dimension Weight)
  • Conservative (0-39) | Moderate (40-69) | Aggressive (70-100)

Course Connection

Original Concept: The RAB Framework was conceived by Bukola Olajire during the Operations Management course at Johns Hopkins Carey Business School (Spring 2026), taught by Graeme Warren.

  • Littlefield Simulation: Capacity-inventory-contract tradeoffs under cash constraints — Financial Risk
  • Neighbors Coffee Shop: Artisanal quality vs. operational efficiency — Strategic Risk
  • Hip Op Hospital: Throughput vs. burnout/quality balancing — Operational Resilience
